The brief

The team came to KMATS with the right ambition and the wrong shape: too many features in the brief, not enough product spine. The first job was to remove ideas, not add them.

What we did

  • Mapped the audience, use case, data dependencies, and success metric.
  • Reduced the feature surface to one workflow worth defending in week one.
  • Designed responsive, dark-mode-aware screens with the engineering constraints in mind.
  • Wired in AI / automation only where it removed real work, not for show.
  • Shipped a build that the internal team could continue without us.

How the work was structured

The engagement ran in three phases: a tight discovery sprint, a focused design + build cycle, and a launch window with measurement turned on from day one.

  1. Discovery (1–2 weeks): users, workflows, AI map, content map, technical map.
  2. Design + build (8 weeks): system, screens, integrations, AI flows, dashboards.
  3. Launch + 30 days: instrumentation, weekly improvement list, handoff documentation.
The product stopped feeling like a list of features and started feeling like a decision the user could make in under a minute.

Outcome

The team launched on schedule with a system they own. Six weeks later, the primary metric moved 3.4× against the previous build. The team kept the playbook and reused it for the next two product moments.

What stayed with the client

  • A component system documented for internal reuse.
  • An AI capability map with bounded responsibilities.
  • A measurement dashboard with the metric they had been avoiding.
  • A 30-day improvement list ranked by effort and impact.
